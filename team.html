Meet the Team

Youth Policy Collective was founded by four high-school students who aspire for change.

Anant Singhal

American School of Bombay
Anant Singhal
Anant is a highschool junior at the American School of Bombay. An MUN enthusiast, he is passionate about international relations and economics. He has previously worked with the Quality Council of India and NITI Aayog on Unnati, a cutting-edge labour portal for blue and grey collar workers.

He has also worked with Stanford Angels & Entrepreneurs, a venture-capital organization run by Stanford alumni and xNav, a startup working in drone and aircraft navigation.

Aisha Tharaney

JNIS, Mumbai
Aisha Tharaney
Aisha is a junior at Jamnabai Narsee International School, Mumbai. She is an avid public speaker and MUN’ner. She has worked with several institutions, authored multiple research papers, interned at a law firm, moderated various discussions and debates and has also led her student body as the head girl of her school. Additionally, she’s the founder of a non-profit that teaches underprivileged students how to play the guitar.

An advocate for equal rights, she truly believes that our generation has the potential to achieve inclusion of the youth’s voice in leadership, policy and practise.

Manya Pratap Singh

The Shri Ram School, Moulsari
Manya Pratap Singh
Manya is a rising junior at the Shri Ram School, Moulsari. Being a passionate photographer, she has started her own photojournalism blog and is a part of her photography editorial board at school.

An aspiring psychology student, she recently worked with the UBC Social Identity Lab to explore the societal biases that lead to gender discrimination in the workplace. Additionally, She is working with the Naz Foundation to help young girls deal with barriers that keep them from achieving their goals such as lack of mobility, gender division of labour etc.

Ultimately, she aims to empower women to achieve their full economic potential and hopes to study behavioral economics in college.

Sarang Phulwaria

Bombay International School
Sarang Phulwaria
Sarang is an 11th-grade student at the Bombay International School. He was elected as the Head Boy and was recently awarded the prestigious Citizenship Award by his school for his service to the community. He has helped raise funds for the underprivileged during COVID-19 with Habitat for Humanity and has participated in several MUNs.

Along with this, he has also taken part in several courses conducted by the Gateway House regarding diplomatic relations of India. He aspires to work in the policy making area for the Indian civil services to help improve the domestic situation for the greater prosperity of our young nation.
